History

I was born and raised in Mexico on September 20, 2002, I grew up with a deep passion for soccer. In 2021, I moved to Phoenix, Arizona, to pursue both my athletic and academic goals at Glendale Community College (GCC), where I played for the soccer team for two years.

My academic journey originally began in Mechanical Engineering, driven by a fascination with automotive design on CAD (Computer-Aided Design) software. However, after taking a Python course and exploring coding on my own, I discovered a new passion for writing code.

After earning my Associate's degree in Computer Science from GCC, I transferred to Grand Canyon University in 2025. I am currently a student in the Software Development program, focusing on mastering languages like Java, C#, and Python while working toward my Bachelor's degree.

Interests

Cars

My interest for cars started when I was 10 years old. One evening, I asked my dad how do cars work, and after his answer, I didn't stop watching videos to know more about them. I get excited when I buy tools to give maintenance to my car.

Soccer

Soccer has played a big role in my life and it has given me incredible experiences. It even made me move to a new country to continue playing at a higher level. If it was not for soccer, I would probably still be living in Mexico.

Fitness

When I was playing soccer for college, I started lifting weights to get stronger, and I loved every session. I currently only play soccer for fun, but I still go to the gym 4-5 times a week because gym has become like therapy.

Software

Coding made me realize I enjoy solving problems and thinking structurally. I always loved spending time on my computer, but programming brought a new purpose to my life after I stopped playing soccer competitively.
